Oasis of Green

Jacob Marquez

One by one my feet hit the ground
Entrenched in an oasis of green
A calm respite from the city around
Better to run in a nature scene
Side-to-side my tired arm swings
A way to escape the stress of life
No worry about school or life things
All around me nature is rife
Truly a sight of pure allure
In a city built from metal, wood, and stone
Shining like when green was pure
When man did not sit on nature’s throne
This oasis shows the importance of green
We must do more to preserve this magic
We need everyone’s help to keep nature pristine
Because any harm to the environment is tragic

Explanation

Last month I ran a half-marathon with my sisters, which I had to train and prepare a lot for. A part of that meant running long distances which I really enjoyed doing at the arboretum. Running on a treadmill did not replicate race conditions well enough, so I had to run outside to properly train. In my opinion, it was much better to run through the arboretum than around campus, due to the privacy, better views, good paths, and it was easier to plan out my mileage. While running there throughout the course of the semester I truly learned to appreciate going outside. Being able to go outside, breathe fresh air, and see a beautiful green space was always a part of my day that I would look forward to. On days when I had to run a particularly far mileage, I would spend up to two hours there. During that time I got to know every path like the back of my hand and even as I would get exhausted the nature around me became my second wind. By looking around at the trees and plants I was able to distract myself and pushed myself to keep going. While I spent my time in the arboretum I was able to start making connections between the park and what we learned in class. I definitely started feeling mentally better after maintaining a workout schedule as well as having a safe space outside of school to decompress. It was a calm and quiet place to get away from the chaos and busyness of college life. No matter how much my life changed I could always count on the arboretum being there. I wasn’t the only one who greatly appreciated the park. I would often see a lot of the same people who were enjoying the park, but also a lot of new people. There were always, a lot of families on the weekends enjoying and learning about nature. From this, it was easy to see the societal impacts of having green spaces near urban communities and how important it is to maintain these spaces. Positive ecological impacts were also very visible. I saw a lot of wildlife and variance in native plant and tree species. This is especially important in an area close to an urban environment where a lot of the natural environment has been disrupted. In this class, we learned all about ecological restoration, and while we had many firsthand experiences, I never felt like I understood the purpose of restoration until spending so much time at the arboretum. I finally was able to see the outcome of all that we were learning about. I noticed everything helped to contribute to being able to have a place as beautiful as the arboretum, and that more people should be educated in it like our class was. I realized how much work and planning had to go into a place as large and purposeful as the arboretum.
